Siege of Orgrimmar

A 10-Man Heroic field manual. Fourteen bosses, four wings, one Warchief — written for guilds learning the fight without a decade of muscle memory.

14 Encounters Heroic Difficulty 10-Player Format

How To Use This Guide

Each boss has two layers. The Summary is what to read 30 seconds before a pull — the one paragraph everyone in the raid should know cold. The Detailed Strategy beneath it is for raid leaders, officers, and anyone preparing assignments: ability breakdowns, phase logic, role-by-role checklists, and the Heroic-specific changes that turn a normal fight into a gear-check.

Composition assumes 2 tanks, 2–3 healers, 5–6 DPS unless noted. Mechanics named in italics are spell names — they will appear in your combat log and your boss-mod (BigWigs/DBM) timers.

Encounter 01 · Pools of Power

Immerseus

A corrupted water elemental, the first gate-keeper.

Wing Vale of Eternal Sorrows
Enrage 10 minutes (hard)
Bloodlust On Pull
Composition 2T · 2-3H · 5-6 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

A two-phase boss that rotates between fighting Immerseus directly and a Split Phase where he shatters into adds. The "Corruption" bar replaces a traditional health bar: every add you kill or heal removes 1 point of Corruption. Get him to 0 to win. Spread evenly around him, avoid the Sha Bolt puddles, swap tanks on Corrosive Blast. Heroic adds a stacking debuff (Swelling Corruption) that spawns adds when you hit him, and a growing Sha Pool in the center of the room.

Encounter Abilities

Immerseus Phase

Sha Bolt Hits every raid member every ~10 seconds, dealing shadow damage and leaving a void zone at their feet. Stay 5+ yards apart; move just out of your zone so the next one drops adjacent (saves space).
Swirl Summons moving void zones AND channels a 10-second water-jet in a cone in front. The cone rotates clockwise. Stay out of the front arc and dodge the wandering zones.
Corrosive Blast Frontal cone every ~35s. Inflicts a stacking 300% increased shadow-damage debuff — tank swap on every cast.
Seeping Sha A ring of water around Immerseus. Don't run through him — you'll be damaged and knocked back. Melee his hitbox edge, not his model.

Split Phase

Sha Puddles (hostile) Kill them. When one dies, players within 10 yards get +25% damage to Sha Puddles, stacking. Cluster your DPS around dying puddles to snowball.
Contaminated Puddles (friendly) Heal them to full. When fully healed, players within 12 yards get +75% healing done AND +25% mana. Healers chain through them.
Center damage Each add that reaches the center pulses raid-wide shadow damage. Kill/heal them before they get there.

Phase Strategy

The fight is a damage race against a soft enrage that you control: more adds dealt with → less Corruption → shorter future Immerseus Phases. The first Immerseus Phase is the longest of the fight, which is why Bloodlust goes on pull — there's nothing better to save it for.

Spread evenly around the boss in a wheel formation. Healers distribute themselves so every spread group has coverage. Tanks face the boss away from the raid and swap on every Corrosive Blast.

Heroic Changes

Heroic Mechanic · Immerseus Phase Swelling Corruption

Once per Immerseus Phase, the boss gains a stacking buff equal to half his current Corruption (so 50 stacks the first time). Every attack removes a stack, gives the attacker a 6-second stacking shadow DoT, AND spawns a Congealed Sha add that meleeing the attacker.

10-man wave strategy: stop DPS at 2–3 debuff stacks per player. Let the adds spawn, kill them, let the DoT fall off and health stabilize, then go again. After the first Swelling Corruption you can usually just push through it normally.

Have a Holy Paladin run Righteous Fury to magnet the Congealed Sha adds for cleave. Without one, your offtank picks them up at a designated stack point.
Heroic Mechanic · Split Phase Sha Pool

A growing void zone covers the center of the arena. It expands over time AND each time an add reaches it. The bigger it gets, the more damage it deals. Solution: rotate raid members with defensive cooldowns through the pool to shrink it. The damage is low per tick, so any class with a personal cooldown can soak.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Face boss away. Swap on every Corrosive Blast.
  • Off-tank picks up Congealed Sha adds during Heroic Swelling Corruption.
  • Stay outside the boss hitbox between threat windows.
Healers
  • Spread for Sha Bolt raid damage; pre-shield if Disc.
  • Chain through Contaminated Puddles — be within 12 yards when one heals to full for the +75% healing buff.
  • Dispel the Swelling Corruption DoT on heavy hitters to extend the wave.
DPS
  • Stand 10 yards from dying Sha Puddles for the +25% damage stack.
  • Manage your own Swelling Corruption stacks — 2–3 max before a pause.
  • Rotate defensives in the Sha Pool during Split Phase.
Do not stand in the center water at the start, or walk through Immerseus to reposition. Seeping Sha will knock you back into a Sha Bolt zone and start a chain death.

Encounter 02 · Pools of Power

The Fallen Protectors

Three corrupted monks — Rook, He, and Sun — fought as a council.

Wing Vale of Eternal Sorrows
Enrage 10 minutes
Bloodlust On Pull
Composition 2T · 2-3H · 5-6 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Three bosses, separate health bars, but they must all die within ~10 seconds of each other or the first to die heals back up. Each one triggers a Desperate Measures phase at 66% and 33% health, disappearing and spawning adds. Coordinate health levels — never let one boss get more than ~5% ahead of the others. Bloodlust on pull.

The Three Bosses

Rook Stonetoe (Tanked) Uses Vengeful Strikes, a frontal cone. Tank faces him away. His Desperate Measures summons three adds: Embodied Misery, Sorrow, and Gloom.
He Softfoot (Mobile) Drops Garrote debuffs and poison void zones. Doesn't need a hard tank — kite him through clean ground. Interrupt nothing of his; everything he does is movement-based.
Sun Tenderheart (Caster) Stands still and casts Sha Sear — interrupt every cast. Drops Calamity void zones.

Desperate Measures (66% / 33%)

Each boss disappears when they hit 66% and 33%. They return when their adds are dealt with.

Rook's Desperate Measures Three Embodied adds + Mark of Anguish

Rook drops Mark of Anguish on a player. That player must pass it (by attacking another) before it expires, or it kills them. Meanwhile Embodied Sorrow, Gloom, and Misery spawn. Kill order: Sorrow → Gloom → Misery. Stack Sorrow and Gloom with He Softfoot for cleave; finish on Misery.

He's Desperate Measures Noise Cancelling

He casts Noise Cancelling — players must move to designated ground markers to break it. Pre-assign positions.

Sun's Desperate Measures Meditation healing

Sun begins channeling Meditation — heals herself and friends rapidly. Interrupt the channel immediately or she'll full-heal.

Heroic Changes

  • All three Desperate Measures fire at once when any boss hits the threshold (not staggered). Plan add priority carefully.
  • The Shadow Word: Bane dispel mechanic from Sun becomes more punishing — multiple players will need to spread before dispel.
  • Tighter execution window between bosses at the end — within 10 seconds means coordinated DPS slowdowns at low HP.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Main tank: Rook, facing away.
  • Off-tank: pick up Embodied Misery during Rook's Desperate Measures.
  • Kite He Softfoot through clean ground; don't tank him still.
Healers
  • Dispel Shadow Word: Bane quickly (after the affected player moves out).
  • Healing CDs during Desperate Measures phases — raid damage spikes.
  • Track passes of the Mark of Anguish — pass receivers take heavy damage.
DPS
  • Match boss health percentages — slow down on whichever is ahead.
  • Assigned interrupters lock down Sun's Sha Sear.
  • Cleave Sorrow + Gloom together for efficient adds.
Use a DBM/BigWigs health-percentage tracker. Call out at 70%/35% to signal "DPS slowdown imminent." Bringing all three to 5% then pushing them together is the cleanest finish.

Encounter 03 · Pools of Power

Norushen

A trial of purity. Manage your Corruption or be consumed by it.

Wing Vale of Eternal Sorrows
Enrage ~10 minutes
Bloodlust At 50% (burn phase)
Composition 2T · 2-3H · 5-6 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

You fight the Amalgam of Corruption, but the real mechanic is the Corruption debuff on each player. High Corruption = massively reduced output. Players use orbs near Norushen to enter a Test Realm where they kill a personal demon to purify themselves (back to 0 Corruption, full power). The boss takes less damage from corrupted players and is empowered by absorbed corruption. Burn it at 50%.

Encounter Abilities

Corruption (debuff) Every player starts at 0. It grows from soaking Residual Corruption orbs, from Self Doubt, and from raid mechanics. High corruption = lower damage/healing done, plus the boss eats your power.
Test Realm Click an orb near Norushen → teleported to a personal realm. Kill the manifestation of your sin, return purified. DPS realms have adds; healer realms need to keep an NPC alive; tank realms test survival.
Blind Hatred A rotating beam from the Amalgam. Dodge it — predictable but deadly. It rotates one direction; pre-position to walk against the spin.
Unleashed Manifestation / Essence Adds that periodically spawn. Kill them fast — they fuse with the boss if ignored, granting buffs.
Expel Corruption Boss launches projectiles. Intercept (stand in their path) to prevent them from reaching the boss — otherwise he gets stronger.

Phase Strategy

The fight is paced by who's been purified. Purify DPS first so they can ramp damage; then tanks (who soak the most corruption from tank mechanics); healers last (they gain Corruption slowest).

Tanks rotate Amalgam threat between active tanking and Test Realm visits. A tank entering the realm needs the other to taunt before they leave; an Unleashed Manifestation can spawn during this swap, so prep cooldowns.

Heroic Changes

  • Quarantine: high-corruption players get Quarantined in a bubble — they take and deal heavy damage to anyone trapped with them. Spread before dispel windows.
  • Burst of Anger: Unleashed Manifestations channel a raid-wide damage spell. Stun and interrupt them — Capacitor Totem, Solar Beam, Shockwave all work.
  • Icy Fear: scales with boss health lost. The burn phase at 50% becomes a healer test.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • One tank starts in Test Realm; other takes Amalgam.
  • Rotate purification — never both purified, never both corrupted at the swap.
  • Soak Residual Corruption orbs when you're already corrupted; it removes them from the floor but gives +25 Corruption.
Healers
  • Stay on the Amalgam tank during pulses; raid damage ramps with boss HP loss.
  • Save cooldowns for 50% burn — Icy Fear raid damage is highest then.
  • Enter Test Realm late; you generate Corruption slowest.
DPS
  • Purify FIRST — go in, kill your demon, come back. Don't tunnel the boss while corrupted.
  • Stun/interrupt Unleashed Manifestations.
  • Intercept Expel Corruption projectiles; missing one buffs the boss.
Lust at 50%. By then most of the raid is purified, the burn phase has started, and the raid damage is highest — cooldowns aligned with healer CDs save the pull.

Encounter 04 · The Scarred Vale

Sha of Pride

The seventh Sha. A fight where your own hubris kills the raid.

Wing Scarred Vale
Enrage ~10 minutes
Bloodlust At 30% (burn)
Composition 2T · 2-3H · 5-6 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Every raider has a Pride resource bar (0–100). Pride is gained from many mechanics — soaking Marks, breaking prisons, dispels, even being too close to other prideful players. At 100 Pride you mind control. Swelling Pride casts periodically, dealing damage scaled to your Pride — low Pride is fine, high Pride explodes you and gives you even more Pride. Manage it carefully, burn at 30%.

The Pride System

Pride is the entire fight. Every player should track it. You GAIN Pride from:

  • Being released from a Projection of Pride prison (+25 Pride).
  • Soaking your Mark of Arrogance on your own (vs. having someone else dispel it).
  • Standing in Pool of Pride void zones (low gain, but it adds up).
  • Some Heroic-specific abilities (see below).

Encounter Abilities

Wounded Pride Stacking debuff on the tank. Swap on each application.
Swelling Pride Cast every ~45 seconds. Detonates everyone — damage scales with your Pride. At 0–25 Pride: minor. At 75+ Pride: deadly AND it gives you more Pride. Spend Pride downward, not upward.
Mark of Arrogance A debuff on a player. Don't dispel it — let it expire (the player soaks it themselves for the +Pride cost). Only dispel if the player is at 100 Pride risk.
Manifestation of Pride Add. Burst it down fast and interrupt.
Projection of Pride (prison) Targets a player and locks them in a translucent bubble. Other players step in to break them out — but the rescuer gains +25 Pride. Rotate rescuers.
Gift of the Titans A buff orb appears periodically. Click it to gain a temporary buff that lets you safely dispel Mark of Arrogance without giving Pride.

Phase Strategy

Single-phase fight that ramps into a burn at 30%. The raid must coordinate Pride at all times: low-Pride players soak rescues, dispel Marks, and take the Gift of the Titans. High-Pride players hide from mechanics until they bleed Pride off.

Heroic Changes

  • Self-Reflection: an extra mechanic where a duplicate of you appears and mirrors your actions. Kill it or it explodes.
  • Aura of Pride: anyone within 8 yards of a high-Pride player gains Pride from proximity. Spread out aggressively.
  • More frequent Swelling Pride.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Swap on every Wounded Pride application.
  • Position the boss in the back of the room facing the throne — leaves room for the raid to spread.
  • Don't soak Marks while actively tanking.
Healers
  • Track everyone's Pride — call out high-Pride players before Swelling Pride.
  • Use Gift of the Titans to safely dispel Marks on high-Pride targets.
  • Heavy raid CDs on each Swelling Pride detonation.
DPS
  • Burst Manifestation of Pride adds immediately.
  • Rotate prison rescues — low-Pride player breaks the bubble.
  • Stand behind the boss to maximize Mastery-style buffs and avoid prison spawns from frontals.
A player hitting 100 Pride gets mind-controlled and will burst the raid down. Track it in DBM. If someone is approaching 90, the raid leader should call for them to step into the closest Projection area or take a Gift of the Titans dispel.

Encounter 05 · Gates of Orgrimmar

Galakras

A proto-drake siege. Razorscale veterans will feel at home.

Wing Gates of Orgrimmar
Enrage Phase 2 ramping aura
Bloodlust Start of Phase 2
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Two-phase fight. Phase 1: kill waves of adds and four mini-bosses on the ground while capturing two towers. Then man both turrets simultaneously to shoot Galakras out of the sky. Phase 2: DPS race on the landed drake while soaking fireballs and managing a stacking damage aura. Lust on phase transition.

Phase 1 — The Siege

Waves of Kor'kron adds spawn at the front, with four named mini-bosses appearing on a rough timer. Two towers (left and right) must be cleared and captured by your raid; the capture bar fills as you kill adds inside.

Korgra the Snake Drops poison clouds. Kite her into clean ground; tank moves her continuously.
Thranok Skullcrusher Chains a player to himself and casts Skull Cracker — chained player runs away to extend the chain and break it.
Krugruk Knockback monster. Position him with a wall behind your tank so you're not flung off ledges.
Drakkari Lord Dagryn Casts Muzzle Spray knockbacks. Tank dances around the tower base to dodge.

Tower Capture

Split into two tower teams — usually 1 tank, 1 healer, 2 DPS each (the remaining group stays at the base for spawns). Clear each tower's adds, then a designated player man-the-turret at the top. Both turrets must fire at the same time to bring Galakras down.

Phase 2 — The Drake Lands

Flames of Galakrond Stacking shadow-fire DoT on the tank. Swap on every application.
Pulsing Flames Raid-wide damage aura that ramps over the phase. This is the soft enrage — beat the boss before it kills you.
Flame Breath Frontal cone. Tank faces away.
Fireball Volley Targets a player with a meteor — players within range share the damage. Soak in groups to split. If only one player is hit, they die.

Phase 2 Positioning

Melee stack behind the boss. Ranged in a tight pile 30 yards back. Fireball Volleys land between the two groups — both stacks together can take it without dying. Tanks swap on Flames stacks.

Heroic Changes

  • Faster mini-boss spawns in Phase 1 — Drakkari Lord can overlap with another miniboss.
  • Phase 2 Pulsing Flames ramps harder. Save raid CDs (Devotion Aura, Spirit Link) for the final 25%.
  • More fireballs in Phase 2 — communicate which group takes which.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • One tank on each tower team (or one stays at base; coordinate before pull).
  • Phase 2: swap on every Flames application — they hurt fast.
  • Pre-position for Krugruk so you're not knocked off the platform.
Healers
  • Spread across both tower teams — 1H per team in 10-man.
  • Save raid CDs for late Phase 2 — Pulsing Flames wipes raids at high stacks.
  • Top up before each Fireball Volley.
DPS
  • Priority adds: Healing Tide Totems > Banners > Demolishers > trash.
  • Stack tight in your group for Fireball Volleys.
  • Phase 2: tunnel boss; do not chase remaining adds unless they're killing the raid.
Have a ranged DPS or healer click the turret in Heroic — they don't stop their DPS/healing rotation entirely. Hunters with auto-shot work perfectly.

Encounter 06 · Gates of Orgrimmar

Iron Juggernaut

Goblin engineering at its most lethal. A two-phase mechanical terror.

Wing Gates of Orgrimmar
Enrage ~10 minutes
Bloodlust On Pull
Composition 2T · 2-3H · 5-6 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Alternates between a 2-minute Assault Phase (mobile, tank swaps on Flame Vents, dodge drill ground effects, detonate crawler mines) and a 1-minute Siege Phase (stationary, raid stacks, kite lasers, dodge mortars). Heroic adds ricocheting sawblades and mortar barrages. Lust on pull.

Assault Phase Abilities

Flame Vents Frontal cone on the tank, stacking debuff. Swap at 3–4 stacks.
Borer Drill A drill chases a random raid member, leaving a ground spike that damages anyone who steps on it. Kite predictably so other players can avoid the trail.
Crawler Mine Spawns and crawls toward a player. They beep faster as they near detonation. Stomp them (run over them) or they explode the raid. Tanks or immunity classes (Cloak of Shadows rogue) take them.
Mortar Cannon Red ground circles. Move out before impact.

Siege Phase Abilities

Shock Pulse Knockback from the boss. Position with a wall behind you or you'll get launched into mortars.
Cutter Laser A targeted laser tracks one player. Run it in a predictable arc away from the raid and away from Explosive Tar patches (laser + tar = pull-ending explosion).
Seismic Activity Raid-wide damage tick. Healing CDs every Siege Phase.
Explosive Tar Pools of tar on the ground. Stay out — when the laser touches them, the room explodes.

Phase Strategy

Position the boss on one side of the room from the start so you have runway for kiting laser/drill. Melee always behind the boss to avoid Flame Vents. Ranged + healers spread for Mortar Cannon but stack loosely in Siege Phase for healing efficiency.

Heroic Changes

Heroic Mechanic · Assault Phase Ricochet (sawblades)

Sawblades launch and bounce around the room. They do not cross terrain, so use the room edges. They become more frequent as the fight progresses.

Heroic Mechanic · Siege Phase Mortar Barrage

Many mortar shells rain down. Stack tightly at the edge of the room after the first Shock Pulse — once you're at the edge, the barrage targets one tight zone that healers can blanket heal through. Sprawling out causes random deaths.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Swap on Flame Vents at 3–4 stacks.
  • Stomp Crawler Mines — coordinate so they don't pile into the raid.
  • Position boss so kited mechanics have lanes.
Healers
  • Heavy CDs every Siege Phase for Seismic Activity.
  • Top up before each Mortar Barrage in Heroic.
  • Spread for Mortar Cannon in Assault, stack for Barrage in Siege.
DPS
  • Laser-target kites away from tar AND away from the raid.
  • Melee: always behind the boss. Ranged: ~30 yards back, spread.
  • Stop DPS to stomp mines if assigned tank can't get there.
Cutter Laser + Explosive Tar = instant wipe. Mark a clear kite path on the floor (a world marker on one side) before the pull and stick to it.

Encounter 07 · Valley of Strength

Kor'kron Dark Shaman

Haromm and Kardris — two shamans, one health pool, escalating chaos.

Wing Valley of Strength (Org)
Enrage Sub-25% damage buff
Bloodlust Pull or sub-25%
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Two bosses (Haromm and Kardris) sharing a health bar, each riding a mount (Darkfang and Bloodclaw — kill these first). The fight gets progressively worse: new abilities unlock at 85%, 65%, and 50%. At 25%, both bosses gain +25% damage and haste. Tank them together; avoid the increasing pile of ground effects.

The Bosses

Earthbreaker Haromm Casts Froststorm Strike on his tank — stacking debuff, swap on each application. Drops Ashen Wall ground effects.
Wavebinder Kardris Drops Foul Stream water trails. Summons Foul Geysers that spawn Foul Slime adds.

Health-Threshold Abilities

100% – 85% Opening Phase

Kill the mounts (Darkfang, Bloodclaw — very low HP). Tank both bosses together on the edge of the room for cleave. Manage Froststorm Strike tank swaps. Avoid Toxic Mist from Kardris.

85% – 65% Adds Unlock

Toxic Storm begins — green clouds wander the arena. Don't get clipped. Healing demands begin to rise.

65% – 50% Foul Slimes

Foul Geyser spawns Foul Slime adds. They explode if killed in melee. Kite them with ranged DPS or Brewmaster Monks (range threat). Avoid Foul Stream trails.

50% – 25% Falling Ash

Ashen Wall drops big diagonal walls of ash you must walk around. Falling Ash rains down — raid CDs every cast. The arena gets crowded — keep bosses moving.

Sub-25% Berserk

Both bosses gain +25% damage and haste. Defensive CDs from everyone. Lust here if you didn't lust on pull.

Heroic Changes

  • Iron Tomb: rooted debuff that requires a friendly attack to break — interrupt your friend, basically.
  • Faster Foul Slime spawns; ranged kite tools become essential.
  • Falling Ash raid damage is significantly higher; mandatory raid CDs each cast.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Tank both bosses together near the room edge for cleave.
  • Swap on each Froststorm Strike.
  • Move bosses constantly to keep the floor clean of effects.
Healers
  • Heavy CDs each Falling Ash cast (50% onwards).
  • Spot-heal Iron Tomb victims (Heroic).
  • Save personal CDs for the sub-25% berserk.
DPS
  • Kill mounts first.
  • Ranged: kite Foul Slimes — don't melee them, they cleave.
  • Break friends out of Iron Tombs (Heroic).
The room becomes more cramped over time. Plan to move bosses counter-clockwise around the perimeter so you always have clean ground.

Encounter 08 · Cleft of Shadow

General Nazgrim

A warrior boss with stances, rage, and waves of his loyal Kor'kron.

Wing Cleft of Shadow
Enrage ~10 minutes
Bloodlust During Berserker Stance
Composition 2T · 2-3H · 5-6 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Nazgrim has a Rage bar. Rage triggers a special ability at 100. He also cycles stances: Defensive (takes less damage, don't hit him), Berserker (takes +25% damage — burst window). Adds spawn in waves: Warshamans heal, Arcweavers cast, Assassins backstab, Ironblades melee. Kill order matters. Lust on Berserker Stance.

Stances and Rage

Nazgrim has three stances, cycling every ~30 seconds. He generates Rage from taking damage and from raid mistakes. At 100 Rage he casts a stance-specific empowered ability.

Defensive Stance Takes reduced damage AND reflects 100% of melee damage back. Only the active tank hits him. DPS focus adds or wait.
Battle Stance Normal damage. DPS the boss.
Berserker Stance Takes +25% damage. Burst window. Use cooldowns.
100 Rage ability Defensive → Defensive Stance reflect; Battle → Heroic Shockwave (cone AoE, spread); Berserker → Bonecracker (heavy bleed).

Adds

Adds spawn in waves throughout. Kill order: Warshaman → Arcweaver → Assassin → Ironblade.

Kor'kron Warshaman Heals Nazgrim and other adds with Chain Heal. Interrupt and kill first. Drops Healing Tide Totem — kill the totem immediately.
Kor'kron Arcweaver Casts Magistrike bouncing chain spells. Interrupt and kill second.
Kor'kron Assassin Stealthed, drops poison clouds, backstabs. Hunters can Flare; otherwise CC and burst.
Kor'kron Ironblade Trash melee. Tank pickup and cleave down.
Kor'kron Banner Drops occasionally — buffs all nearby adds with War Song. Kill instantly when it appears.

Tank Mechanics

Sundering Blow Stacking debuff on the tank — armor shred. Swap at 2+ stacks.
Ravager (Heroic) Nazgrim throws a weapon that spins on the floor. Don't walk through it — it gives him Rage.

Heroic Changes

  • Execute: Below 20% boss HP, casts Execute on the tank every ~15 seconds. Mandatory tank cooldowns for each one.
  • Snipers: Range adds that fixate a player and cast Multi-Shot (cone). Face them away from the raid.
  • More frequent add waves; the soft enrage is add accumulation.

Phase Strategy / Final 10%

Below 10%, the strategy changes: ignore adds, lust, kite them to the back of the room. The boss is the only thing that matters. If you burn him fast enough, the adds never close the gap.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Swap on 2+ Sundering Blow stacks.
  • Off-tank picks up Ironblades and Snipers.
  • Tank CDs on EVERY Execute in Heroic.
Healers
  • Heavy AoE healing during add waves (cleave damage from Magistrike).
  • CDs on Heroic Shockwave and Bonecracker.
  • Top up tank pre-Execute.
DPS
  • Stop hitting Nazgrim in Defensive Stance — it reflects damage and gives him Rage.
  • Strict add priority: Warshaman → Arcweaver → Assassin → Ironblade. Banner = instant.
  • Lust on Berserker Stance for the +25% damage.
Hitting Nazgrim during Defensive Stance is the #1 cause of new-group wipes. Stop your rotation. Mark it on a /raid macro: "STANCE — STOP DPS".

Encounter 09 · The Underhold

Malkorok

An ogre fueled by Y'Shaarj's blood. A pure execution test.

Wing The Underhold
Enrage ~7 minutes (tight)
Bloodlust On Pull
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Two phases. Might of the Kor'kron (2 min): players have absorb-shields instead of normal healing — dodge frontals, soak Imploding Energy orbs to clear them. Blood Rage (20 sec): stack and split heavy damage. Tank swap on Fatal Strike. Tight enrage — Lust on pull. Heroic adds Living Corruption and Orbs of Corruption.

Might of the Kor'kron Phase (2 minutes)

Ancient Miasma (absorb shield) Healers cannot direct-heal. Instead they build up Ancient Miasma shields on each player. The shield is what soaks damage. Healers spam to keep everyone shielded. Take damage → eat shield → if no shield, you die.
Arcing Smash Frontal cone. Tank faces away; rest of raid stays out of the front.
Breath of Y'Shaarj Marks zones on the ground. Get out before impact — they leave persistent void zones.
Imploding Energy Spawns void orbs that must be soaked by players. The soaker takes the damage (absorbed by shield). Pre-assign soakers — one per orb. If unsoaked, raid-wide damage.
Fatal Strike Tank attack with a stacking debuff. Swap at 2–3 stacks.
Seismic Slam AoE around the boss. Stay spread 5+ yards.

Blood Rage Phase (20 seconds)

Boss casts Blood Rage. Heavy raid-wide damage. Stack tightly so the damage is shared evenly. The Ancient Miasma shield system continues. Healers go all-out shielding.

Displaced Energy Debuff during Blood Rage. The affected player explodes when it expires/dispelled. Move 8+ yards from the raid before it pops.
Relentless Assault Each Blood Rage Phase, Malkorok gains a permanent damage buff. This is the soft enrage — kill him in 3 Blood Rage cycles or it spirals.

Heroic Changes

  • Living Corruption: adds spawn periodically. Kill them fast or they explode.
  • Orbs of Corruption: float around. Use immunities (Cloak of Shadows, Ice Block, Divine Shield, Hand of Protection) to clear them. They cause heavy raid damage if untouched.
  • During Blood Rage, raid may need to move through Malkorok to avoid stuck rooted Displaced Energy players.

Phase Strategy

The fight has a brutal effective enrage built into Relentless Assault. Each Blood Rage Phase makes damage worse. Lust on pull and try to push enough damage to limit yourself to 3 Blood Rage cycles.

Pre-assign Imploding Energy soakers. Pre-assign Orb clearers (immunity classes). Healers should rotate cooldowns by Blood Rage cycle.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Swap on 2–3 Fatal Strike stacks.
  • Face boss away from raid for Arcing Smash.
  • Survive Blood Rage with personal CDs — shields don't protect tanks reliably.
Healers
  • Spam Ancient Miasma shields. Prioritize soakers and tanks.
  • Disc Priests and Resto Shamans excel here.
  • Save raid CDs (Spirit Link, Tranquility) for Blood Rage.
DPS
  • Soak your assigned Imploding Energy orb.
  • Move 8+ yards out for Displaced Energy before it pops.
  • Immunity classes clear Orbs of Corruption (Heroic).
Healers cannot heal normally — they generate absorb shields only. If someone takes damage and has no shield, they die. Shield uptime is the entire fight.

Encounter 10 · Kor'kron Barracks

Spoils of Pandaria

No boss. A timed crate-clearing race against two factions.

Wing Kor'kron Barracks
Enrage Energy timer (per side)
Bloodlust Final quadrants if tight
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS (1 of each per side)
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Split the raid into two teams: one fights Mogu adds, one fights Mantid adds, in mirrored arenas. Open crates to spawn adds; killing adds builds an Energy bar to 50. Then a lever opens the next quadrant. Race the timer. Open Pandaren Crates for raid-wide buffs.

How It Works

The room has two halves connected by a central corridor: Mogu side (right) and Mantid side (left). Each half has a series of quadrants. In each quadrant, three crate types sit on the floor:

  • Lightweight: spawns 1 add. Quick energy, low risk.
  • Stout: spawns 2 adds. Solid energy.
  • Massive: spawns several adds. High energy, high risk.
  • Pandaren: spawns a friendly NPC. Granting a raid-wide buff (Brewmaster: tank damage buff; Mistweaver: healer procs; Windwalker: DPS procs). Open these FIRST.

Killing adds fills the Energy bar to 50 per quadrant. At 50, you can pull the lever to open the next quadrant. Both sides must complete simultaneously or the laggard side wipes the raid.

Mogu Side Mechanics

Mogu Shadow Ritualists Cast Forbidden Magic. Interrupt every cast.
Mogu Elders + Stone Statues Spawn Stone Statues. Kill the statues — they buff the Elder otherwise.
Anima Golems Cast Matter Scramble beams between two players. Both stand in BOTH beams (yes, really) to share the damage.
Crimson Reconstitution (Heroic) A healing pool on the floor for adds. Tank drags mobs out; standing in it damages players in Heroic.

Mantid Side Mechanics

Set to Blow Player gets a debuff and drops bombs on the floor that will detonate. Drop them in empty space; don't trigger them.
Amber Priest Casts Residue debuff. Dispel.
Wind Wielder Casts Rage of the Empress. Dispel; interrupt.
Kor'thik Warcaller Enrages when low HP. Kite during enrage.

Heroic Changes

  • Unstable Sparks: spawn when crates are cleared. Ranged DPS focus them down immediately — they explode the raid otherwise.
  • Crimson Reconstitution: now damages players standing in it (not just healing).
  • Tighter energy/timer windows. Cleave crates open in pairs (lightweight + stout) for efficient energy generation.

Strategy

Split raids cleanly: 1 tank, 1 healer, 2–3 DPS per side. Each side needs a designated caller. Pandaren crates open first. Calculate energy in advance — 2 Massive crates = 28 Energy, then a Stout (6) gets you near. Don't overpull and overshoot energy with the next quadrant's adds.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • One per side. Strong AoE threat.
  • Mogu tank pulls mobs OUT of Crimson Reconstitution.
  • Mantid tank kites Enraged Warcallers.
Healers
  • Dispel-heavy specs preferred (Resto Druid, Holy Priest).
  • Watch for Residue (Mantid) and Torment (Mogu).
  • 3rd healer floats — covers whichever side is rougher.
DPS
  • Ranged kill Unstable Sparks (Heroic) on sight.
  • Interrupters lock down Shadow Ritualists and Wind Wielders.
  • Cleave lightweight crates alongside larger ones.
Coordinate lever pulls with the other side. Whichever side hits 50 Energy first waits at the lever for the other side to be ready, then both pull together.

Encounter 11 · The Menagerie

Thok the Bloodthirsty

A devilsaur with three captives — he eats them, and steals their power.

Wing The Underhold (Menagerie)
Enrage Phase 2 stacking
Bloodlust On Pull
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Two-phase fight. Phase 1: tank Thok, manage his stacking attack speed, dodge his frontals. Phase 2: he fixates on raid members; kite him to a Kor'kron Jailer NPC; kill the jailer to free one of three captives (Akolik, Gorai, Montak); Thok eats the captive and gains their abilities, cycling back to Phase 1 with new mechanics. After all three are eaten, you're locked in Phase 2 forever — wipe. Kill him before that.

Phase 1

Bloodthirsty (haste) Thok's attack speed ramps over the phase. Tanks die faster as it stacks. Healers gear up.
Acid Breath Frontal cone. Tank faces away.
Tail Lash Rear cone. Don't stand behind him.
Deafening Screech Channeled spell. Do not cast spells during this — interrupted casts heal Thok and damage the caster.

Phase 2

Fixate Thok runs at random players. He's slow but deadly. Kite him in a planned circuit.
Kor'kron Jailer NPC A jailer guards each captive. Kill the jailer to free a captive. Thok will eat the freed captive, restoring Phase 1.

The Three Captives (Phase 1 Variants)

After eating Akolik Acid Breath empowered

Phase 1 abilities all gain acid components. Tank takes acid DoTs. Raid stacks for cleave healing.

After eating Gorai Ice Tombs

Thok periodically encases players in Ice Tombs. Break them out fast — the trapped player takes huge damage. Raid stacks for AoE break.

After eating Montak Fire void zones

Fiery ground effects appear. Spread out — opposite of the other two variants.

Captive Order (Heroic)

Most groups choose Akolik first (easiest variant), then Gorai, leaving Montak for last (hardest, but the fight should be near-dead). Pre-plan and stick to it.

Heroic Changes

  • Higher Bloodthirsty haste growth — Phase 1s must be shorter.
  • Phase 2 fixates faster.
  • Captive abilities are more punishing — ice tombs hit harder, fire pools persist longer.

Phase Strategy

Lust on pull to burn through Phase 1 cleanly. The raid's job is to extend Phase 1 with defensive cooldowns (Bloodthirsty ramps so DPS does the most early). Phase 2 is short — kite cleanly, kill the jailer, get back to Phase 1.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Face Thok carefully — frontal cone AND tail lash.
  • Swap on debuff stacks if you get them (variant-specific).
  • Pre-plan Phase 2 fixate kiting paths.
Healers
  • Do NOT cast during Deafening Screech.
  • Save raid CDs for high Bloodthirsty stacks.
  • Burst-heal ice tomb victims (Gorai variant).
DPS
  • Burst Phase 1 hard while Bloodthirsty stacks are low.
  • Phase 2: switch to the Kor'kron Jailer immediately.
  • Break ice tombs in Gorai variant; spread in Montak variant.
Three captives = three Phase 1s = three soft enrages. If you can't kill Thok before the third Phase 2 ends, you wipe. Track your kill timeline against the captive count.

Encounter 12 · The Siegeworks

Siegecrafter Blackfuse

Goblin assembly-line warfare. The hardest fight in the raid.

Wing The Siegeworks
Enrage ~10 minutes
Bloodlust On Pull
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

A conveyor belt at the edge of the room delivers four types of weapon adds. A dedicated conveyor team kills one weapon per wave before it reaches the boss (he gets empowered if any reach him). The main raid tanks the boss and an Automated Shredder off-boss, dodges sawblades, dodges shockwaves, kites lasers. Universally regarded as the toughest mechanical fight in Siege.

Encounter Layout

The room is rectangular with a conveyor belt along one long edge. Weapons travel along it. Where they reach the end without being killed, Blackfuse overcharges — empowering whatever variants he uses afterwards. The conveyor belt has a Matter Purification Beam that will instantly kill anyone who touches it on the belt.

The Four Weapons

Crawler Mine Walking bombs. Highest priority kill. They blow up the raid if they leave the belt. Stun/snare them, focus down.
Laser Turret Targets a player with a laser. The laser must be kited away from the raid. Heroic: rotating fire rings.
Missile Turret Fires Shockwave Missiles with orange ground indicators. Move out.
Electromagnet Pulls sawblades toward it. Useful — pulls sawblades off the floor. Don't kill it every wave; rotate so sawblades get cleared.

Conveyor Belt Strategy

Assign 2 DPS to the belt (alternate waves — a 60s debuff prevents reuse). They jump onto the belt, burst one weapon, jump off. Hunters can Disengage back off the belt cleanly. Priority each wave:

  • Crawler Mines always die first. They're the most disruptive.
  • Let an Electromagnet through occasionally to clear sawblades.
  • Avoid letting two Laser Turrets through back-to-back in Heroic.

Boss Mechanics

Electrostatic Charge Stacking debuff on the tank. Swap on stacks.
Automated Shredder Mini-boss that off-tank kites 35+ yards from Blackfuse. If they overlap, the Shredder heals the boss.
Sawblades Periodically launched, leave persistent ground trails. Force the raid to slowly migrate.
Shockwave Missile From Missile Turrets — orange indicator, move out.

Heroic Changes

  • Overcharged adds: when a weapon reaches the boss, it stays as an empowered variant on him. Splitting Crawler Mines, ring-firing Laser Turrets, etc. Mistakes compound.
  • Tighter conveyor timing — Crawler Mines spawn faster.
  • Mortar Cannon's red zones are larger.

Phase Strategy

The boss has no phases. The fight is a logistical puzzle: manage two parallel demands (belt team + main raid) while sawblades slowly compress your usable space. Tank the boss in the back corner so you can drift forward as sawblades accumulate.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Main tank: Blackfuse, in the back corner, swap on Electrostatic stacks.
  • Off-tank: kite Automated Shredder 35+ yards from boss.
  • Plan your migration as sawblades accumulate.
Healers
  • Heavy raid healing — frequent AoE damage from missiles and laser kites.
  • Pay attention to belt team — they spike in damage during their wave.
  • Disc Priest shields excel here.
DPS
  • 2 belt DPS (mobile classes preferred — Hunter, Mage, Rogue).
  • Laser-targeted DPS kite the laser away from sawblades and raid.
  • Tunnel boss between belt waves; never let the boss healing get out of hand.
This is the boss where pre-pull diagrams matter. Mark world markers on the floor: belt team jump-on point, shredder kite zone, laser kite arc, sawblade safe zones. Verbal calls during the pull aren't enough.

Encounter 13 · Chamber of the Paragons

Paragons of the Klaxxi

Nine bosses. You fight three at a time. They keep coming.

Wing Chamber of the Paragons
Enrage ~12 minutes
Bloodlust On Pull (burn Skeer)
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

Nine Klaxxi bosses, but only three are active at once. Kill one → another joins. Each dead Paragon leaves a corpse buff the raid can pick up. Order matters. Heroic order: Skeer → Rik'kal → Korven → Iyyokuk → Ka'roz → Hisek → Xaril → Kaz'tik → Kil'ruk. Long fight, encyclopedic in mechanics.

How the Encounter Works

The fight begins with three active Paragons. When you kill one, the next from a fixed order activates. The dead one leaves a corpse that any player can click for a unique buff — these are critical to progress.

The Paragons (Heroic Kill Order)

1. Kaz'tik the Manipulator → kill SKEER first No, you fight Skeer first. Skeer drops Bloods that must be killed. Lust on Skeer.
Skeer the Bloodseeker Drops Blood adds. Tank debuff. Burst him first with Lust.
Rik'kal the Dissector Casts Injection: stacking debuff on tank. Summons Amber Parasites — kill them fast.
Hisek the Swarmkeeper Casts Aim: a charging line attack — ranged stack on the line to share damage; line takes ~3 sec to charge, must position. Sonic Discharge: dispel.
Korven the Prime Encase in Amber: locks a player in amber. Free them by attacking the block. Hewn tank debuff.
Iyyokuk the Lucid Drops Fiery Edge — spread to avoid stacking damage. Calculation: math puzzle, players need to be in specific groupings.
Ka'roz the Locust Whirling: spinning slash damage; avoid stacking. Gives Strong Legs buff that lets the raid break Amber blocks.
Xaril the Poisoned Mind Three poison colors (red/blue/yellow): each demands a different reaction — red = move, blue = stack, yellow = spread. Healers must recognize the color and call out.
Kaz'tik the Manipulator Mesmerize: charms players. Use stuns to break. Hungry Kunchong: pet, kill during Mesmerize.
Kil'ruk the Wind-Reaver Final boss. Death from Above: leap targeting a player — spread. Reave: tank cleave.

Corpse Buffs (Critical)

Each dead Paragon leaves a buff. Assign these intelligently:

  • Ingenious (from Iyyokuk) — healers love it.
  • Master of Amber (from Korven) — tanks for damage reduction.
  • Strong Legs (from Ka'roz) — DPS for movement to break amber blocks.
  • Cyclone (from Ka'roz pre-death) — get out before he dies.

Heroic Changes

  • Strict kill order: deviations from Skeer → Rik'kal → Korven → Iyyokuk → Ka'roz → Hisek → Xaril → Kaz'tik → Kil'ruk make later combinations brutal.
  • Rapid Fire (Hisek): faster Aim casts.
  • Active paragon abilities don't stop while you're killing others — managing 3-at-a-time mechanics late-fight is the test.

Phase Strategy

This is the longest fight in the instance. Pace yourself. Lust on Skeer (first kill) for momentum. The middle paragons (Korven, Iyyokuk, Ka'roz) are about pure mechanic execution. The final paragons (Xaril through Kil'ruk) demand peak coordination — by then, the raid is tired, so simplifying assignments and pre-calling each mechanic is essential.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Swap on every Paragon's tank debuff (each has one).
  • Pick up adds (Bloods, Parasites, Kunchongs) — they multiply.
  • Take Master of Amber corpse buff for damage reduction.
Healers
  • Dispel Sonic Discharge, Diminish, Xaril's poisons.
  • Track ALL active paragon timers — heavy raid damage stacks across them.
  • Take Ingenious corpse buff.
DPS
  • Follow the kill order; don't tunnel a single boss.
  • Burst adds (Bloods, Amber Parasites, Kunchongs) immediately.
  • Use Strong Legs to break Amber prisons.
Print out the kill order. Tape it to your monitor. Call each transition out loud: "SKEER DEAD, RIK'KAL ACTIVE — TANK PICK UP PARASITES".

Encounter 14 · The Inner Sanctum

Garrosh Hellscream

The Warchief, fed by the heart of an Old God. The grand finale.

Wing Inner Sanctum / Vision of Stormwind
Enrage Phase 4 ramping
Bloodlust Phase 4 (Heroic)
Composition 2T · 3H · 5 DPS
Wowhead reference

The Summary

A four-phase fight (Heroic gets a bonus Phase 4 in a ruined Stormwind). Phase 1: tank Garrosh, kill adds, use Iron Stars against him. Phases 2/3: alternate between fighting Garrosh in the real world and the Realm of Y'Shaarj where you slow his power. Mind controls, whirlwinds, mounting raid damage. Heroic Phase 4: transported to ruined Stormwind for the final burn — Iron Star kiting, Malice soaking, no margin for error. Lust on Phase 4. Trinket: the Kor'kron Juggernaut mount.

Phase 1 — Garrosh and Iron Stars (100% → 10%)

Desecrate Periodically Garrosh casts Desecrate on a player, leaving a desecrated weapon on the floor. Don't stand on it — long-lasting void zone.
Annihilate Frontal cleave. Melee stay behind boss.
Iron Stars Giant rolling Iron Stars cross the room. Use them: knock them into adds or into Garrosh himself (huge damage). Avoid getting hit yourself. Heroic: triggered when 4+ players group up.
Kor'kron Warbringers Adds. Off-tank picks up, raid cleaves them down.
Wolf Riders Adds that cast Chain Lightning. Do NOT interrupt — interrupting triggers a damage buff. Just kill them; the cast is weak.

Phase Transitions (Realm of Y'Shaarj)

At 10%, Garrosh becomes invulnerable, heals to full, and starts absorbing the Heart of Y'Shaarj. The raid alternates between two states:

Real World Fight Garrosh, suffer escalation

Garrosh casts increasingly damaging mechanics each cycle. Adds keep spawning. Mind controls begin.

Realm of Y'Shaarj Slow his corruption

Designated players are teleported. Kill Minions of Y'Shaarj there to slow Garrosh's energy gain back in the real world. Adds empower Garrosh if they melee, so kill them efficiently.

Phases 2 & 3 — Empowered Mechanics

Whirling Corruption Garrosh spins, summoning projectiles. Spread out so projectiles spread; standing close = bursts of damage.
Touch of Y'Shaarj Mind controls a player. Pre-assign stuns per player: Bash, Hammer of Justice, Quaking Palm. A controlled player nukes the raid with full rotation. Improvising = wipe.
Empowered Whirling Corruption Heroic Phase 3. Spread is mandatory — projectiles also leave persistent voids.
Gripping Despair Stacking tank debuff. Swap at 8–10 stacks (the exact swap point depends on your tanks' gear).

Heroic Phase 4 — Vision of Stormwind

Heroic and Mythic only. When Garrosh hits 0% (he's invincible at this point), 20 seconds of RP. Then the raid is teleported to a ruined Stormwind. He heals to 60%. Final burn.

Malice (Malicious Blast) Garrosh fires a beam at a player and stacks a debuff. Successive hits to the same player are deadly. Rotate players in/out of the beam using immunity cooldowns (Ice Block, Divine Shield, Cloak of Shadows, Bubble) to reset Malice stacks.
Manifest Rage Cast at 100 energy. Heavy raid damage AND summons many Manifestation of Rage adds. Interrupted by Iron Star impacts — kite Iron Stars to crash into Garrosh.
Bombardment / Iron Stars (Phase 4) More Iron Stars. Designate kiters (Druids, Priests with Body and Soul) to lead them into Garrosh during Manifest Rage casts.

Heroic Strategy Highlights

  • Pre-assign stun rotation for every player in case of Touch of Y'Shaarj. Document it. Rehearse it.
  • Iron Star kiters in Phase 4: usually 1 Druid and 1 Priest with movement buffs. They lead stars into Garrosh.
  • Malice soakers: a rotation of immunity-equipped players cycle the beam.
  • Warlock Gates connect both ends of the room for crossing during add waves.
  • Battle res carefully: don't res a player while they're in the Realm of Y'Shaarj.

Phase Strategy / Pacing

Phase 1 is a controlled DPS race — get him to 10% before mechanics compound too far. Transitions are mostly add-management efficiency. Phases 2/3 are coordination tests: spread, stun, dispel, repeat. Phase 4 (Heroic) is the wall — it tests every system: tank cooldowns, healer mana, DPS rotation, raid-leader call timing.

Role-By-Role Checklist

Tanks
  • Tank Garrosh on the Horde crest in the center; off-tank handles Warbringers.
  • Swap on 8–10 Gripping Despair stacks (Phase 2/3).
  • Phase 4: heavy tank damage — every CD on rotation, especially when moving the boss.
Healers
  • Raid CDs on every Whirling Corruption.
  • Track Malice stacks; pre-heal Malice receivers.
  • Save defensives for Manifest Rage casts in Phase 4.
DPS
  • Stun assignments for Touch of Y'Shaarj — execute on schedule.
  • Do NOT interrupt Wolf Rider Chain Lightnings.
  • Iron Star kiters use them as boss interrupts in Phase 4.
  • Phase 4 burn: Lust here. Tunnel.
A missed stun during Touch of Y'Shaarj = a fully-geared raider nuking your healers for 5+ seconds. Pre-assigned stun rotations are non-negotiable. Improvisation does not work on Heroic Garrosh.
Heroic Garrosh drops the Kor'kron Juggernaut mount and Tusks of Mannoroth transmog — 1% rates each. They are removed when MoP Classic ends. Every kill counts.
